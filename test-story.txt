The old man sat at the edge of the pier, his fishing line disappearing into the dark water below. He had been there since dawn, and the bucket beside him was still empty.

A girl appeared on the boardwalk behind him, maybe ten years old, carrying a paper bag. She watched him for a long moment before speaking.

"You're not going to catch anything," she said.

He didn't turn around. "That's what they told me yesterday too."

"Did you catch anything yesterday?"

He smiled at the water. "No."

She sat down next to him, opened her paper bag, and pulled out a sandwich. She tore it in half and held one piece out toward him without saying anything.

He took it. They ate in silence, watching the line that never moved.

After a while she said, "My grandfather used to fish here."

"I know," the old man said. "I'm him."

She looked at him — really looked — and something in her face changed. Not surprise exactly. More like a puzzle piece finding its place.

"Mom said you were gone."

"I was." He reeled in the empty line and cast it out again. "But the fish keep not biting, so I keep coming back."

She laughed. It was a sound that made the seagulls jealous.
